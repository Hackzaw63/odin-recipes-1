The well cooked pancetta, fresh ingredients, and delicious béchamel leave nothing to be desired as this wonderful meal assaults your taste buds. Being quite easy to create just follow the below recipe instructions and send your taste buds to a new wonderland.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. ground beef
  • 1 lbb ground beef chuck (20% fat)
  • kosher salt, freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 oz pancetta
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled, finely chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3/4 cup dry white wine
  • 28 oz can whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup whole milk

Béchamel and Assembly

  • 7 Tbsp. unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup plus 3 Tbsp. all-purpose flour
  • 6 cups whole milk
  • 4 oz parmesan cheese, coarsely grated (about 1 cup)
  • pinch of cayenne pepper
  • pinch of ground nutmeg
  • Kosher salt, freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 1/4 lbs dried lasagna noodles (preferably De Cecco)
  • Extra-virgin olive oil (for greasing)

Now here comes the fun part of making all of this delicious food! The Cooking!

Let's Get Cooking

Ragù Bologones (meat sauce)

  1. Preheat oven to 225°. Mix 2 lbs of ground pork and 1 lb ground beef chuck (20% fat) with your hands in a large bowl; season generously with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, then mix again. Form into 18 large meatballs.

  2. Warm 2 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oul in a large Dutch oven or other heavy pot over medium-high heat, Working in 2 batches, cook meatballs, turning occasionally and reducing heeat if bottom of pot looks in danger of scorching, unit browned all over, about 6 minutes per batch. Transfer to a rimmed baking sheet as they're done.

  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add 2 oz pancetta, to pot and cook, stirring often, until lightly browned and beginning to crisp, about 5 minutes. Add 1 medium onion, 1 celery stalk, 1 medium carrot, amd 6 cloves of garlic. Stirring occasionally until vegetables are sofetened 6-8 minutes. Add 2 Tbsp tomato paste and cook, stirring constantly untill paste is darkened in color, about 2 minutes

  4. Add ¾ cup dry white wine and cook, stirring occasionally, until almost completely evaporated, about 5 minutes. Add one 28-oz. can whole peeled tomatoes, crushing with your hands, and increase heat to medium-high. Cook, stirring occasionally, until liquid is jammy and reduced by about half, 8–10 minutes. Add 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth and 1 cup whole milk, then return meatballs to pot. Bring to a simmer. Cover pot partially with a lid, transfer to oven, and cook, checking every hour or so to ensure liquid is at a low simmer and adjusting oven temperature as needed, until meatballs are falling-apart tender, 3–4 hours.

  5. Using a potato masher, break meatballs apart and incorporate into liquid (you should have about 8 cups ragù; transfer pot to stovetop and simmer gently to reduce if needed). Taste and season with more salt and black pepper if needed.

    6. Do Ahead: Ragu can be made 4 days ahead. Let cool; cover and chill, or freeze up to 3 months

Béchamel and Assembly

  1. Melt 7 Tbsp. unsalted butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add ¼ cup plus 3 Tbsp. all-purpose flour and cook, whisking constantly, until mixture is pale golden, about 4 minutes.

  2. Gradually whisk in 6 cups whole milk. Bring to a simmer and cook, whisking often, until mixture is thickened and coats the back of a spoon, 8–10 minutes.

  3. Remove from heat and gradually whisk in 4 oz. Parmesan cheese, 1 cup at a time, until melted and smooth; season with a pinch of cayenne pepper and a pinch of ground nutmeg. Taste and season with more salt if needed.

  4. Do Ahead: Béchamel can be made 4 days ahead. Let cool; press plastic wrap directly onto surface and chill.

  5. Preheat oven to 375°. Cook 1¼ lb. dried lasagna noodles in a large pot of boiling salted water, stirring occasionally, until very al dente. Drain and rinse under cold water.

  6. Lightly coat a 13x9" baking dish with extra-virgin olive oil. Spread a thin layer of ragù (about 1 cup) over bottom of dish. Top with a layer of noodles, cutting to fit as needed. Spread a third of the remaining ragù over noodles. Dollop a third of the béchamel over ragù in small spoonfuls; spread evenly. Repeat layers of noodles, ragù, and béchamel twice more. Top with a final layer of noodles, a final layer of ragù, and a final layer of béchamel. Scatter 4 oz. Parmesan cheese over béchamel.

  7. Cover lasagna with foil and bake until bubbling and beginning to brown around the edges, 45 to 50 minutes. Remove foil and continue to bake until top is browned and bubbly, 15–20 minutes longer. Let sit 15–30 minutes before serving.
